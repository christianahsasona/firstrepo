Groupers

Groupers are teleosts (ray-finned fishes), typically having a stout body and a large mouth. They can be quiet large, and lengths over a meter and weights upto 100 kg are not uncommon. though obviously in such a large group, species vary considerably. They swallow prey rather than biting pieces off it. They do not have many teeth on the edges of their jaws, but they have heavy crushing tooth plates inside the pharyx. They habitually eat fish, octopuses, and crutaceans. Reports of fatal attacks on humans by the largest species, the giant grouper (Epinephelus Lanceolatus) are unconfirmed.

Their mouth and gills form a powrefull sucking system that sucks tjeir prey in from distance. They also use their mouts to dig into sand to form their shelters under big rocks, jetting it out through their gills. Their gill muscles are powerful, it is nearly impossibkle to pull them out of a cave if they feel attacked and extend those muscles to look themselves in.
